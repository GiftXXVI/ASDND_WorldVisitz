WorldVisitz Agile Proposal

Background: Current Situation

  • The development team collects requirements at the beginning of the project and write a specification. They validate the specification with business users, freeze the specification and use it as input to design.
  • At this point, they freeze the requirements and use them to create designs for the business logic and user experience.They also validate these with the users.
  • Then they freeze the design and implement it, test the implementation, fixing errors and deploy the tested implementation for the business users to validate, accept and adopt it.

Background: The Downside

  • The first version of the system will only be seen after 18 months.
    • If we realize that some requirements have changed or some things are missing or we have added excess features, we can only realize and fix it after 18 months.
    • At any point past the validation of each phase, you will not be able to request changes to the requirements.
    • If we identify opportunities for improvement, to add business value through the application, we will not be able to capitalize on them during these 18 months.

Background: The Solution

  • We switch to an Agile Methodology.
    • We will build the application in iterations, with each iteration producing a new increment of our application.
    • We will empower our developers to discuss work among themselves and allocate and deliver it while sharing expertise among themselves.
    • With this schedule, we will start seeing parts of the application and business value immediately after the first increment.
    • As we see more of the application, we will discover incorrect assumptions and implementations and we will correct it in future increments
    • Eventually, we will have an application that matches business requirements and is able to adapt rapidly to new business requirements.

Agile Methodologies

Agile methodologies are based on the Agile Manifesto. They have increased focus on:

  1. Engagement with business users.
  2. Delivery of business value.

What is the Agile Manifesto?

In 2001, a group of software developers met to find common ground among their methodologies. This common ground became the Agile Manifesto. The Manifesto says they value:

Individuals and interactions Over processes and tools
Working software comprehensive documentation.
Customer collaboration contract negotiation.
Responding to change following a plan.

Why we should follow the Manifesto

  • It recognises that:
    • People build software with their domain knowledge and technical skills.
    • Working software brings the most business value of all project deliverables.
    • Requirements are bound to change over time and as our understanding improves.
  • Having the right priorities leads to consistent, predictable and repeatable success.

Long Before Agile, There was Waterfall

  • In the waterfall methodology, requirements are collected from customers, stakeholders and users at the beginning of a project and a fixed linear sequential schedule of phases is followed to design, create, test and deploy the product.

The problems with Waterfall

  • Once requirements are established, they become a contract between the developer(s) and the customer and it is almost impossible to change them.
  • The output of each phase is a milestone that terminates the current phase and serves as the (required) initial input to the next phase.
    • At the end of each phase, the milestone(or deliverable) is reviewed.
    • If a milestone is approved, the work of the phase is not redone. Even if requirements change.

How Agile Fixes Waterfall's Problems

  • In contrast Agile allows and anticipates changes in requirements at any point in the development process.
  • Agile also includes Business Users in the developement team and encourages collaboration and constant communication thus feedback is immediate.
  • Agile is incremental and iterative by design so there are many reviews to learn from and there are many opportunities to incorporate feedback into future iterations.

When to use Waterfall (and Agile)

  • Waterfall works well when both the customer's requirements and technology for implementation are well known.
  • Agile is recommended if either:
    • the requirements are not well understood
      • or
    • the technology is not well known
  • If both requirements and technology are unclear, first clarify at least one.

How will WorldVisitz benefit from Agile?

  • Agile uses iterations to deliver small increments of the whole application, learning from the feedback of previous increments to make future increments and improve previous increments
  • This ensures that at least part of the application is delivered early and that the customer starts seeing the system and receiving business value from it early.

Agile Frameworks: Kanban

  • Kanban is based on Lean Product Development.
  • Lean Product Development and Kanban aim to minimise waste and maximize productivity.
  • Visualization of work and progress is very important in Kanban.
  • A Kanban team focuses on work in progress and requires minimization of number of items in work in progress to match the capacity of the team.

Agile Frameworks: Scrum

  • Scrum is a lightweight framework that helps people, teams and organizations generate value through adaptive solutions for complex problems.
  • Scrum is founded on:
    • Empiricism: learning from experience.
    • Lean Thinking: minimising waste.
  • Pillars of Scrum:
    • Transparency: the work must be visible to the developers and the clients.
    • Inspection: as they observe the work, developers and clients must inspect the product to detect defects in the product.
    • Adaptation: as variations and defects are detected, the product must be modified to correct them.
